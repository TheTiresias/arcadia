By afternoon she had made a decision, though she could not have said exactly when it happened. Decisions of that kind don't arrive with fanfare. They surface, already made, and you recognize them the way you recognize your own handwriting.

She packed lightly. A bag, a book, the charger she always forgot.^[She did not forget it this time. This is how you know something has changed.]

## The Road

The highway was empty in the way highways get empty in the middle of the day — not deserted, but purposeful, everyone moving too fast to notice anyone else. She kept to the right lane and let them pass.
